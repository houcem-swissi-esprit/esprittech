Esprit-Tech

Created in 2010, ESPRIT-Tech is the Research-Development-Innovation (RDI) structure at ESPRIT.

Since the establishment of the training programs, the RDI activity at ESPRIT has become one of the priorities in the strategic choices.

Through this entity, ESPRIT faculty are interconnected and can benefit from funding and support opportunities, including grants, RDI training, partnership development, contract negotiation, as well as commercialization assistance. ESPRIT-Tech develops strategies, and future directions for RDI activities, in close collaboration with relevant stakeholders. Furthermore, it gives special priority to applied research and innovation, without excluding basic research.

Structure

ESPRIT-Tech is a research structure led by a director who oversees all strategic and operational aspects.

In order to carry out its activity, ESPRIT-Tech has set up a structure that responds to its mission: teams are created in order to carry out RDI projects around themes and research axes. Students can be associated to these works through their end of studies projects.

ESPRIT-Tech created in December 2015 a research committee: C-RDI (RDI Committee), composed of all the team leaders. It is responsible for boosting research activities and ensuring the quality of productions. It meets on average once a month.

For a better functioning, the RDI Committee has a secretariat (SC-RDI) composed of 5 members who meet regularly. Its role is to solve problems by applying the decisions taken by the C-RDI.

Our Mission

Vision

In order to strengthen the school’s vision, ESPRIT-Tech will continue to expand research, development, innovation and funding opportunities, facilitate excellence in applied research and promote the commercialization of the results of RDI projects.

Mission

ESPRIT-Tech’s mission is to provide faculty with an environment conducive to the development of research and innovation and a full range of high-quality services in support of their projects, in order to increase external funding for RDI activities and contribute to the construction of a national ecosystem, open to the international, and based on an entrepreneurial economy.

Values

  • Scientific rigor and integrity
  • Collaboration and knowledge sharing
  • Respect
  • Commitment and social responsibility
  • Inclusivity
  • Transparency
  • Sustainability
